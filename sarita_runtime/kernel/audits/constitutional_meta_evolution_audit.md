# Constitutional Meta-Evolution Audit (Phase 105.1)

## Overview
This audit evaluates the current state of SARITA's evolutionary architecture to transition from local constitutional optimization to global civilizational supremacy.

## Analyzed Components

### 1. ConstitutionalGenome
- **Status**: Functional for single-constitution gene representation.
- **Limitation**: Lacks "meta-genes" that define civilizational archetypes and governing axioms for entire families of constitutions.

### 2. ConstitutionalFitnessEngine
- **Status**: Measures local fitness and stability (P_s).
- **Limitation**: Optimized for local adaptation (MCE-1). It does not measure long-term civilizational viability or cross-universe stability.

### 3. ConstitutionalEvolutionEngine / ConstitutionalSearchEngine
- **Status**: Efficiently searches the local constitutional design space.
- **Limitation**: Lacks meta-evolutionary layers. It finds the "best" local constitution but doesn't explore alternative "constitutional civilizations" (MCE-2).

### 4. AdaptiveEnvironmentEngine
- **Status**: Simulates environmental stressors.
- **Limitation**: Simulates a single "universe" at a time. It cannot parallelize or compare distinct evolutionary multiverses (MCE-4).

### 5. MultiGenerationEvolutionEngine / LongTermDominanceEngine
- **Status**: Tracks lineages up to 500 generations.
- **Limitation**: Target horizon is too short for civilizational supremacy (needs 5000+). Does not model civilizational extinction vs. dominance at scale.

### 6. DominanceProofEngine
- **Status**: Generates proofs for local superiority.
- **Limitation**: Proofs are grounded in local fitness metrics. It lacks the GCSI (Global Civilizational Supremacy Index) required for MCE-5.

## Gap Identification

| Gap ID | Description | Severity |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **MCE-1** | **Local Optimization**: System focuses on the highest local fitness peak rather than exploring the global landscape of civilizational archetypes. | Critical |
| **MCE-2** | **Absence of Competing Civilizations**: Evolution is monolithic; there is no infrastructure for parallel, competing civilizational models. | Critical |
| **MCE-3** | **Absence of Meta-Constitutions**: The genome is the constitution itself, not the *generator* of constitutional rules. | High |
| **MCE-4** | **Absence of Universe Comparison**: Lack of a multiverse framework to test how different meta-rules perform across 10,000+ unique starting conditions. | High |
| **MCE-5** | **Absence of Verifiable Supremacy**: No formal theorem or index (GCSI) proves that a civilizational model is mathematically dominant over all others. | Critical |

## Conclusion
The current architecture is highly optimized for Phase 90-104 requirements but requires a fundamental shift to "Meta-Evolution" (Phase 105) to achieve Civilizational Supremacy.
