# Constitutional Identity Audit - SARITA Phase 97

## Component Analysis

### ConstitutionalPurposeEngine (Phase 94)
- **Findings**: Goals are defined and aligned, but they can be modified.
- **Gaps**: No distinction between "Peripheral Goals" and "Identity-Defining Goals". A reform could change the fundamental mission of SARITA without triggering an identity violation.

### ConstitutionalValueEngine (Phase 95)
- **Findings**: Values objectives based on utility.
- **Gaps**: Utility can favor adaptation over identity. A more "efficient" identity might be selected over the original, core SARITA identity.

### ConstitutionalFitnessEngine (Phase 96)
- **Findings**: Maximizes survival probability.
- **Gaps**: "Survival" currently means "the system keeps running". It doesn't mean "SARITA keeps running as SARITA". Extinction is avoided, but mutation is uncontrolled.

### SovereignPreservationEngine (Phase 96)
- **Findings**: Creates structural reserves for continuity.
- **Gaps**: Preservation is focused on material continuity (keeping threads/authority alive), not ontic continuity (keeping the constitution's essence alive).

## Detected Gaps
1. **Ontological Drift**: No mechanism to measure if SARITA (t=N) is ontologically equivalent to SARITA (t=0).
2. **Essence Vulnerability**: Fundamental principles (Unicity, Evidence, Sovereignty) are not marked as "Invariants".
3. **Identity-Blind Selection**: Evolutionary selection favors Fitness (P_s) over Identity (I_c).
4. **Ontological Erasure**: Extreme survival scenarios could lead to the deletion of "expensive" core principles.
